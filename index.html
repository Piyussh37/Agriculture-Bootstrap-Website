Planting

Mulching

Mowing

Plowing
Planting is the foundational step in agriculture, where seeds are sown in soil to initiate crop growth, setting the stage for future cultivation. Mulching involves covering soil with organic materials to conserve moisture, suppress weeds, and moderate temperature, promoting healthier plant growth and reducing maintenance. Plowing turns over the soil, aerating it, breaking up compacted areas, and removing weeds, preparing it for planting and enhancing soil fertility and structure. Mowing trims grass or vegetation to a desired height, improving appearance and health by encouraging even growth and reducing the risk of pests and diseases.

Seeding

Fresh Vegetables

Watering

Vegetables Selling
Seeding involves spreading seeds over prepared soil, ensuring even distribution and covering them lightly to facilitate germination and the establishment of crops. Fresh vegetables represent the successful harvest of crops, offering a bounty of nutritious, locally grown produce that is ready for consumption and enjoyment. Watering provides essential moisture to plants, supporting their growth and development. Regular watering is crucial for maintaining healthy plants and achieving a successful harvest. Vegetable selling is the final stage where harvested produce is sold to consumers. This step allows farmers to earn income and connect their products with buyers.
